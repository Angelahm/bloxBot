Intro to Arduino

Lesson 1 What is an Arduino and how do I get started?

Arduino is a microcontroller which means that it's like the brains of the machine. It accepts input from the environment through sensors and gives output through the instructions to -turn on a light or power a motor so your robot can run across the floor.

The Makey Makey we worked with earlier is preprogrammed with instructions.

Arduino has to be programmed but it works with Windows, Mac or Linux

To read more introduction go to: http://www.arduino.cc/en/Guide/Introduction

You will need to create a place to write and store programs and instructions for your arduino to carry out. This place in your computer is called an integrated development environment or IDE. It's like a note pad to wrote directions on for the machine.

First you need to download and open an IDE.

If you are in Windows or Mac go here to download your version: http://www.arduino.cc/en/Main/Software

If you are running a Distro of Linux always check your computer's software center first. There are many types of software you can typically download at no cost and they are from vetted sources unless you have added a source.

I easily found mine in the software center by searching for Arduino.
