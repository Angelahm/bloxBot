Intro to Makey Makey

Makey Makey is a micro controller that does not need programming. There are spots to plug wires into the board which are labeled to interact with the mouse, keyboard, sound output. It is the best starter micro controller I have found so far. Students who experiment with this will learn about circuits and conductivity.

Circuits

In order for electricity to do a job the current has to run in a circuit, sort of like a circle. The power leaves the source and travels back to the source. Even when you plug in a lampone wire of the two wire cord carries fresh energy in and the old energy travels down the second cord and back to its source.

Image credit: Think Geek

Conductivity

Conductivity is a measure of how easily electricity flows through an object. If electricity flows easily the item is a conductor if the electricity does not flow well then the item is a insulator. There are several wells to test conductivity but to stay safe an get a comparable measurement you need an OHM meter. But you do not have to have this tool to work with your makey makey.

Day 1: Build a Banana piano

  1. You can use this video to explain what a MakeyMakey is: http://youtu.be/1m2iQ4fws8Y
  2. Walk through the steps of What is a Makey Makey and what can I do. http://makeymakey.com/
  3. Build a banana piano following these instructions: http://makeymakey.com/piano/
  4. If it works, play away.
  5. If not, trouble shoot:
    • Is the micro USB plugged in to the Makey Makey and is the USB end plugged into the keyboard?
    • Is your alligator clip attached to a pair of “earth” ports?
    • Are the wires plugged into different bananas?
    • Are the other ends of the wires plugged into the the keyboard, WASDFG?

Day 2: game controller or music with pencil lead

  1. This video will show some examples of pencil lead musical instruments: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3MLkvqB5lSo
  2. Test for pencil lead conductivity.

  3. Take a variety of pencils and use an ohm meter to test for conductivity using the scientific method. Choose the pencil with the greatest conductivity and carefully draw your circuits to the edge of your page connect the alligator clips to them. If you cannot find a pencil lead which conducts well you can use squishy circuits play dough to build a circuit. You can find the recipe here: http://courseweb.stthomas.edu/apthomas/SquishyCircuits/conductiveDough.htm Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3MLkvqB5lSo

  4. Set up your game controller and plug alligator clips into either your UP, Down, Left, right and space or plus your wires into the keyboard slots.
  5. Start up your game and play
